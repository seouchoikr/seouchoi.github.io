Overview

My research in quantum optics studies how quantum fluctuations and nonlinear optical dynamics can be used to realize useful physical functionality. A central focus is the optical parametric oscillator, where the interplay between gain, dissipation, and vacuum fluctuations produces rich quantum and stochastic behavior.

I am particularly interested in how these effects can be measured, controlled, and translated into experimentally relevant tools, including intracavity quantum-state tomography and ultrafast optical pulse characterization.

Research Directions

This line of work investigates quantum-to-classical transitions in optical parametric oscillators, the dynamics of quantum states generated inside nonlinear cavities, and biased cavity systems whose output statistics can be tuned by vacuum fluctuations and external control.

More broadly, the goal is to understand how quantum optical systems can serve not only as testbeds for fundamental physics, but also as platforms for sensing, tomography, and unconventional optical information processing.
