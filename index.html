Seou Choi

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at MIT, where I work on optical computing, quantum optics, and nanophotonics. My recent research focuses on photonic probabilistic machine learning using quantum vacuum noise, intracavity quantum-state dynamics, and nanophotonic devices for X-ray imaging.

I received my M.S. in EECS from MIT in 2024 under the supervision of Prof. Marin Soljacic. Before joining MIT, I earned a B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Seoul National University in 2022, graduating summa cum laude.

About

My work aims to connect physical insight with scalable photonic systems. I am interested in how nonlinear and quantum optical phenomena can be harnessed for computation, learning, and sensing, and in how nanophotonic design can improve the performance of imaging hardware. Across these directions, I try to develop methods that are both physically grounded and experimentally relevant.

Research Interests

Optical computing, quantum optics, and nanophotonics.

Representative topics include photonic probabilistic machine learning, optical parametric oscillators, quantum-to-classical dynamics in nonlinear cavities, metasurfaces, and nanophotonic scintillators for high-resolution X-ray imaging.

Selected Research Projects
Photonic Probabilistic Machine Learning Using Quantum Vacuum Noise
Seou Choi, Y. Salamin, C. Roques-Carmes, R. Dangovski, D. Luo, Z. Chen, M. Horodynski, J. Sloan, S. Z. Uddin, and M. Soljacic
Nature Communications, 2024

This work demonstrates a photonic hardware platform for probabilistic machine learning that uses quantum vacuum noise as a native source of randomness, connecting optical physics with scalable stochastic computation.

Quantum-State Dynamics in Nonlinear Optical Cavities
Seou Choi, Y. Salamin, C. Roques-Carmes, J. Sloan, M. Horodynski, and M. Soljacic
Nature Communications, 2025
Article link to be added

I study how quantum states evolve inside nonlinear optical cavities and how biased optical parametric oscillators can be used for intracavity quantum tomography and pulse characterization.

Nanophotonic Scintillators for X-ray Imaging
Seou Choi and collaborators
Selected papers in Nature Communications, Light: Science & Applications, and ACS Photonics, 2025-2026
Article links to be added

This research introduces nanophotonic scintillators and related wavefront-engineering strategies for X-ray imaging, with the goal of improving spatial resolution, energy resolution, and thermal management in practical imaging systems.

Topologically Protected All-Optical Memory
Seou Choi, J. Kim, J. Kwak, N. Park, and S. Yu
Advanced Electronic Materials, 2022
Article link to be added

My earlier work explored robust optical memory in nonlinear photonic systems, showing how topological protection can be used to stabilize all-optical information storage.

Seou Choi, H. Son, and B. Lee
Optics Express, 2021
