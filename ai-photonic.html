Overview

Photonic hardware offers a promising route toward high-throughput and energy-efficient computing. My research in this area studies optical hardware as a platform for machine intelligence, with an emphasis on computing paradigms that go beyond conventional deterministic digital systems.

A central theme of this work is probabilistic and quantum-inspired photonic computation. In particular, I investigate how physical sources of optical randomness, nonlinear cavity dynamics, and device-level photonic design can be used to implement computational primitives relevant to machine learning and inference.

Research Directions

This theme includes photonic probabilistic machine learning using quantum vacuum noise, optical parametric oscillator systems for stochastic and quantum-state dynamics, and related device concepts for future optical AI hardware.

The goal is to connect physical mechanisms in photonics with computational functionality: how optical systems can represent uncertainty, perform probabilistic state evolution, and support scalable learning architectures with favorable speed and energy characteristics.
