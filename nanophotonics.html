Overview

My research in nanophotonics focuses on how nanoscale structuring of optical materials can be used to control light generation, propagation, and collection in practical devices. A major application area is X-ray imaging, where nanophotonic design can improve the performance of scintillators and related imaging components.

I am interested in device concepts that combine optical design with real experimental constraints, including spatial resolution, energy resolution, absorption, emission, and thermal management.

Research Directions

This work includes nanophotonic scintillators for X-ray imaging, wavefront engineering for scintillation-based imaging, photonic-crystal strategies for supercollimation, and end-to-end design of multicolor scintillators for improved spectral and imaging performance.

The broader goal is to engineer light-matter interaction at the nanoscale in ways that lead to more capable imaging hardware, including higher resolution and lower radiation dose in next-generation detector systems.
